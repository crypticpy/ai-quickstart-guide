# Slide 3: Build When Control Is The Point

Main point: Build internally when integration, data control, auditability, or reusable platform learning matters.

Bullets:

- Sensitive or unusual workflows
- Deep integration with agency systems
- Need for reusable modules
- Strong internal or partner engineering capacity

Speaker notes:

Building does not mean building everything from scratch. It may mean assembling approved services under agency control.

Image guidance:

Internal platform blocks connected to agency systems.

Evidence and review notes:

Confirm internal capacity before recommending build.

---

# Slide 4: Buy When The Market Is Mature

Main point: Buying can be practical when the use case is standard and the vendor can meet agency guardrails.

Bullets:

- Commodity capabilities
- Clear security and data terms
- Exportable logs and records
- Acceptable contract language
- References at similar scale

Speaker notes:

Buying is not skipping governance. It shifts the review to procurement, contract terms, security, data rights, and exit plans.

Image guidance:

Vendor questionnaire checklist.

Evidence and review notes:

Do not include vendor names unless locally verified and approved.

---

# Slide 5: Partner When Learning Is Shared

Main point: Partnerships can help when the agency needs expertise, evaluation support, or shared infrastructure.

Bullets:

- Peer jurisdictions
- State or regional consortia
- Universities or civic tech partners
- Existing shared-service providers

Speaker notes:

Partnership is not a way around procurement, privacy, or security. It works best when roles, data rights, and ownership are explicit.

Image guidance:

Network of peer agencies and partners around a shared project.

Evidence and review notes:

Verify partnership authority and data-sharing rules locally.

---

# Slide 6: What To Defer

Main point: Some opportunities should wait until governance, data, staffing, or procurement are ready.

Bullets:

- Unclear data rights
- No named business owner
- High public impact with weak review
- Vendor refuses basic guardrails
- Integration cost exceeds value

Speaker notes:

Deferring is a real decision. It protects capacity and prevents the first AI program from being defined by the loudest pitch.

# Slide 7: Vendor Pitch Triage

Main point: Vendor pitches should be reviewed quickly and consistently.

Bullets:

- Keep
- Defer
- Decline
- Needs more information

Speaker notes:

Use the current vendor pitch list. Spend 60 to 90 seconds per pitch. The goal is not final procurement evaluation; it is executive direction.

Image guidance:

Four-column triage board.

Evidence and review notes:

Use only approved internal vendor list; do not publish sensitive procurement details.

# Slide 8: Lock-In And Exit Costs

Main point: AI procurement should include exit planning from the beginning.

Bullets:

- Data export
- Prompt and workflow portability
- Model substitution notice
- Audit log access
- Contract-end deletion

Speaker notes:

The agency should avoid buying a capability it cannot leave, inspect, or govern. Exit terms are part of cost.

Image guidance:

Checklist labeled "Before we sign."

Evidence and review notes:

Align with procurement guardrails and counsel review.
