# Slide 3: Augmentation vs. Automation

Main point: Most Year 1 AI work should augment staff work, not automate consequential decisions.

Bullets:

- Augmentation: AI helps a human do work
- Automation: AI performs work with less direct human involvement
- Most first uses should keep humans in the loop
- Higher-risk automation needs deeper review

Speaker notes:

Use concrete examples from the room. A drafting assistant that helps a person write a first draft is augmentation. A system that sends a denial, enforcement notice, or benefit decision without meaningful human review is a different risk category.

# Slide 4: What May Get Faster

Main point: AI is often useful for draft, summarize, search, format, translate, and organize tasks.

Bullets:

- First drafts and rewrites
- Summaries of long materials
- Search across approved documents
- Formatting and routine conversion
- Preparing options for human review

Speaker notes:

Make clear that faster does not mean final. The person still reviews, edits, and owns the work.

# Slide 6: What Becomes Possible

Main point: The Desire-stage question is what staff could do better if repetitive support work takes less time.

Bullets:

- Better follow-through
- More prevention and outreach
- More quality review
- More mentoring and learning
- Better resident service

Speaker notes:

Do not promise that every job gets easier. Ask staff what work has been crowded out by volume, rework, or paperwork. This is where the session moves from "what changes" to "why this may be worth trying."

# Slide 10: What Makes A Good First Try

Main point: Staff should start with low-risk, reviewable tasks.

Bullets:

- Uses public or approved data
- Does not affect rights or benefits
- Has an easy human review step
- Saves time even if edited
- Teaches something useful

Speaker notes:

Connect this to the AUP and intake process. If the task involves sensitive data or consequential outcomes, staff should ask before trying it.

# Slide 11: What To Avoid

Main point: Staff should avoid unsanctioned tools, sensitive data, and unreviewed outputs.

Bullets:

- Do not paste sensitive data into unapproved tools
- Do not treat output as verified truth
- Do not skip human review
- Do not use AI where policy says no

Speaker notes:

Keep the tone coaching-oriented, not punitive. Staff need clear boundaries and safe ways to ask questions.
