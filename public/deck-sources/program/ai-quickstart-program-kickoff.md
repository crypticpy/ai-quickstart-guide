# Deck Source: AI Quickstart Program Kickoff

## How to use this file

Paste or upload this Markdown into an AI presentation tool such as Gamma, Beautiful.ai, ChatGPT, Claude, PowerPoint Copilot, or another deck-generation workflow. Ask it to create a first-draft kickoff deck for an AI quickstart program.

Before presenting, replace placeholders, align with the agency's approved scope, confirm local governance status, apply agency branding, and review for accessibility.

## Deck instructions

- Audience: agency staff, working group members, department leaders, or an internal steering group
- Meeting length: 30-45 minutes
- Desired deck length: 12 slides
- Tone: practical, calm, transparent, non-hype
- Format: 16:9 presentation
- Use speaker notes: yes
- This is a kickoff and alignment deck, not a technical training deck
- Do not invent authority, legal requirements, budget approvals, agency commitments, or tool approvals
- Preserve the message that the checklist is a flexible first-quarter operating plan, not a rigid mandate

## Localization checklist

- Replace `[Agency]`, `[executive sponsor]`, `[program lead]`, `[working group]`, `[start date]`, and `[quarter]`.
- Confirm the sponsor memo, working group roster, and program scope.
- Confirm whether the AUP, Review Committee, intake form, and Approved AI Tools List exist yet or are still in draft.
- Confirm the current communication channel for questions.
- Add agency branding only after content review.

# Slide 2: What This Quickstart Is

Main point: The quickstart is a practical operating plan for the first quarter of AI adoption.

Bullets:

- 13 weekly steps
- Roughly one calendar quarter
- About 65 business days
- Flexible sequence, not a deadline

Speaker notes:

Explain the timing carefully. The quickstart is designed around a quarter of work time. Agencies can compress or extend based on legal, procurement, security, budget, council, or staffing constraints.

# Slide 3: What This Quickstart Is Not

Main point: The quickstart does not require a major production AI launch in the first quarter.

Bullets:

- Not a mandate to adopt every AI tool
- Not a replacement for legal or security review
- Not a promise that AI will solve every backlog
- Not a production launch deadline

Speaker notes:

This slide protects trust. The goal is durable structure: policy, intake, training, sandbox planning, and a reviewed starter-project shortlist.

# Slide 4: Why We Are Starting Now

This is the paint-by-numbers view. If the agency already has one step done, do not redo it for ceremony. Confirm it, update it if needed, and move forward.
