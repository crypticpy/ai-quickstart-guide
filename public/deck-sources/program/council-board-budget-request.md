# Deck Source: Council Or Board Budget Request

## How to use this file

Paste or upload this Markdown into an AI presentation tool such as Gamma, Beautiful.ai, ChatGPT, Claude, PowerPoint Copilot, or another deck-generation workflow. Ask it to create a first-draft budget request deck from the slide-by-slide source below.

Before presenting, replace placeholders, rebuild the budget with current local assumptions, route through finance/legal/procurement review, apply agency branding, and review for accessibility.

## Deck instructions

- Audience: council, board, executive sponsor group, budget committee, or approving body
- Meeting length: 10-20 minute presentation plus questions
- Desired deck length: 10 slides
- Tone: plain-language, fiscally responsible, non-hype, public-sector appropriate
- Format: 16:9 presentation
- Use speaker notes: yes
- Do not invent budget figures, ROI, legal requirements, vendor claims, savings, incidents, citations, or approvals
- Keep claims defensible and local
- Use charts only when the agency supplies the data

## Localization checklist

- Replace `[Agency]`, `[amount]`, `[fiscal year]`, `[approving body]`, `[agency head]`, `[program lead]`, and `[starter use case]`.
- Rebuild cost estimates using current pay bands, contracts, provider pricing, procurement assumptions, and finance guidance.
- Re-run the ROI calculator for the selected use case.
- Confirm whether the request is for funding, procurement authority, staff allocation, policy approval, or all of these.
- Route through legal, finance, procurement, IT/security, and executive sponsor review before public presentation.

# Slide 1: AI Program Funding Request

Main point: [Agency] is requesting [amount/action] to launch a governed, practical AI quickstart program.

Bullets:

- Request: [amount/action]
- Period: [fiscal year/period]
- Sponsor: [agency head or executive sponsor]
- Scope: governance, training, approved tools, and one starter use case

Speaker notes:

Open with the request in the first minute. Do not start with AI hype. The approving body should immediately know what action is being requested.

Image guidance:

Clean title slide with agency branding and a simple public-service workplace image. No futuristic imagery.

Evidence and review notes:

Confirm the requested amount and authority before presenting.

# Slide 2: Why Now

Main point: The agency needs a reviewed path because AI use and vendor pressure are already arriving.

Bullets:

- Staff are encountering AI tools now
- Vendors are adding AI features to existing products
- Unmanaged use creates data and procurement risk
- A structured program channels demand into review

Speaker notes:

Keep the framing balanced. The issue is not whether AI is good or bad in the abstract. The issue is that the agency needs rules, training, review, and accountability before use spreads.

Image guidance:

Simple pathway from "unmanaged use" to "governed use." Avoid alarmist visuals.

Evidence and review notes:

Use local examples only if verified and approved for public discussion.

# Slide 3: What The Funding Buys

Main point: The request funds operating capacity, not just software.

Bullets:

- Governance: policy, risk tiers, review committee, procurement guardrails
- Staff readiness: training for staff, managers, leaders, and reviewers
- Approved tools: controlled access and support
- Starter use case: one reviewed project with human oversight

Speaker notes:

This slide should make clear that AI cost is not only licenses. The request funds the ability to use AI safely and practically.

Image guidance:

Four-block investment map: governance, readiness, tools, starter project.

Evidence and review notes:

Confirm the final funding categories with finance.

# Slide 4: The Cost Model

Main point: The budget should show people, services, tooling, and external review separately.

Bullets:

- People: [amount]
- Cloud and AI services: [amount]
- Tooling and licenses: [amount]
- External services and review: [amount]
- Total Year 1 request: [amount]

Speaker notes:

Use numbers from the agency's budget worksheet, not generic figures. If using ranges, explain what drives the low and high ends.

Image guidance:

Simple stacked bar or table using only agency-supplied numbers.

Evidence and review notes:

Rebuild all figures using current pay tables, contracts, provider pricing, procurement assumptions, and finance guidance.

# Slide 5: Expected Benefit

Main point: The benefit case should be conservative, measured, and tied to a specific starter use case.

Bullets:

- Starter use case: [starter use case]
- Primary benefit driver: [hours saved / rework avoided / cycle time reduced]
- Estimated annual benefit: [amount]
- Estimated payback: [months]

Speaker notes:

Avoid broad AI productivity claims. Use one use case and show the math. If the agency does not yet have measured baselines, label the benefit as an estimate and commit to updating it after launch.

Image guidance:

Small value model: baseline, change, measured benefit, update after launch.

Evidence and review notes:

Use the ROI calculator output or finance-approved model. Do not invent savings.

# Slide 6: Risk Controls

Main point: The program includes controls before AI use scales.

Bullets:

- Acceptable Use Policy
- Risk classification and review path
- Vendor questionnaire and procurement addendum
- Human review for consequential outputs
- Audit logging and production readiness sign-off

Speaker notes:

This is where the approving body sees that the request is not "buy AI and hope." The program invests in controls that make AI adoption reviewable and supportable.

Image guidance:

Simple control chain: policy, intake, review, procurement, launch, monitoring.

Evidence and review notes:

Confirm which controls are approved, draft, or planned before presenting.

---

# Slide 7: What We Will Not Do

Main point: The funding request should set boundaries as clearly as it sets ambitions.

Bullets:

- No unapproved tools for sensitive agency data
- No autonomous high-stakes decisions
- No public-facing launch without readiness review
- No vendor pilot that bypasses procurement and security review

Speaker notes:

This slide builds trust. It names the agency's restraint. Adjust the bullets to match local policy and counsel guidance.

Image guidance:

Boundary-setting slide with simple check and stop icons. Keep the tone calm.

Evidence and review notes:

Confirm all boundaries with legal, procurement, security, and executive sponsor.

# Slide 8: First-Quarter Deliverables

Main point: The agency will report tangible progress before asking for broader scale.

Bullets:

- Sponsor memo and working group roster
- Draft or approved AUP and risk tiers
- Review Committee charter and intake form
- First training cohort
- Starter-project shortlist and quarterly readout

Speaker notes:

Tie this to the quickstart checklist. The first quarter is about durable structure and evidence, not a major production system.

Image guidance:

Quarterly milestone checklist.

Evidence and review notes:

Confirm which deliverables are already complete and which are planned.

# Slide 9: How Progress Will Be Reported

Main point: Sponsors and the approving body should receive short, regular reporting on cost, progress, risks, and asks.

Bullets:

- Quarterly milestone report
- Use-case pipeline and risk tiers
- Spend vs. budget
- ROI estimate vs. actuals
- Incidents, blockers, and decisions needed

Speaker notes:

This slide addresses accountability. Reporting should include asks and risks, not just accomplishments.

Image guidance:

Mock quarterly report dashboard using placeholder numbers only.

Evidence and review notes:

Confirm reporting cadence and audience before presenting.

# Slide 10: Decision Requested

Main point: The approving body is asked to authorize a specific, bounded next step.

Bullets:

- Approve: [amount/action]
- Authorize: [role] to proceed under the governance framework
- Require: quarterly reporting to [body/sponsor]
- Next milestone: [date or gate]

Speaker notes:

End with the decision in the same language that appears in the agenda item, memo, or resolution. If the action is not a vote, adjust to "direction requested" or the local term.

Image guidance:

Decision card with fields for action, owner, reporting, and next milestone.

Evidence and review notes:

Confirm decision language with the clerk, counsel, finance, or local approving process before presenting.
