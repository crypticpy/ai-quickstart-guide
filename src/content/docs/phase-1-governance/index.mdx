## Objective

Establish the guardrails that enable everything else. Without governance, infrastructure requests stall, vendors can't be evaluated, and any public-facing AI deployment carries unmanaged political risk.

## How this phase scales for your agency

<Tabs syncKey="agency-size">
<TabItem label="Small">

**2-day governance sprint with external counsel.** Hire a contract attorney with public-sector AI experience for a focused two-day intensive that produces the AUP, the 3-tier risk classification, and the committee charter. Adopt the templates as-is rather than redrafting from scratch.

- **Deliverables:** AUP (adopted), risk classification (adopted), committee charter (signed), procurement addendum (attached to standard contract template).
- **Skip for now:** Standing review committee meetings (run ad-hoc instead), legislative compliance matrix (refer to the published one rather than maintaining your own).
- **Budget:** $3K–$8K (single sprint engagement).
- **Off-ramp:** This is itself a complete deliverable — most legislative requirements are satisfied by what comes out of the sprint.

</TabItem>
<TabItem label="Standard">

**Five working sessions across Months 1–3.** Convene a 6–8 person Review Committee (tech lead, legal, security, ethics, business sponsor, public representative) and run them through the full deliverable list using the templates in this phase.

- **Deliverables:** Everything listed below — 3-tier risk classification, AUP, committee charter, procurement guardrails, legislative compliance matrix, use case inventory.
- **Cadence:** Committee meets every other week through the launch period, monthly thereafter.
- **Budget:** $5K–$25K (staff time + external legal review).

</TabItem>
<TabItem label="Large">

**Full sessions plus standing committee with sub-groups.** Run the same five working sessions, but split the committee into two working groups (policy + procurement) that meet in parallel. Add a quarterly compliance review and an annual policy refresh.

- **Deliverables:** Everything in the Standard path, plus jurisdiction-specific procurement guardrails, multi-jurisdiction compliance matrix (if you operate across states), and an annual policy refresh process.
- **Cadence:** Standing committee meets monthly; sub-groups meet weekly during launch.
- **Budget:** Skews toward the high end of $25K and into the dedicated policy-counsel range.

</TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Deliverables

- 3-tier AI risk classification (Low / Medium / High) with examples mapped to agency operations
- AI Acceptable Use Policy (based on Mississippi/ISACA templates, adapted for agency context)
- AI Review Committee charter (6–8 members)
- AI procurement guardrails (per OMB M-25-22)
- Legislative compliance matrix (Texas TRAIGA, Colorado AI Act, California SB 942, etc.)
- AI use case inventory template (aligned with OMB M-24-10)

## Off-ramp

Agencies that complete only this phase walk away with an adopted AI policy, risk classification, and review committee. That alone satisfies most legislative requirements and is a complete, standalone outcome.

## What's in this phase

- [3-Tier Risk Classification](/phase-1-governance/risk-classification/) — matrix, 15 government examples, classification workflow
- [AI Acceptable Use Policy](/phase-1-governance/acceptable-use-policy/) — fillable AUP based on Mississippi/ISACA reference templates
- [Review Committee Charter](/phase-1-governance/review-committee/) — composition, authority, decision rules
- [Procurement Guardrails](/phase-1-governance/procurement-guardrails/) — OMB M-25-22 contract addendum, vendor questionnaire, red flags
- [Legislative Compliance Matrix](/phase-1-governance/legislative-compliance/) — federal + state AI law mapped to Phase 1 artifacts

## Suggested order

1. **Adopt the Risk Classification Matrix first.** Every other Phase 1 artifact references the tiers.
2. **Stand up the Review Committee** — charter, members, first meeting on the calendar. The committee will own everything else.
3. **Draft and adopt the AUP.** The committee enforces it; the matrix gives it tier-by-tier teeth.
4. **Attach the Procurement Addendum** to your standard contract template before the next AI procurement.
5. **Localize the Legislative Compliance Matrix** for your jurisdiction and update your AUP's `{{Applicable State AI Law}}` placeholder.

You can run steps 1–3 in parallel if you have the people. Step 4 depends on legal sign-off; step 5 is a quarterly maintenance task once started.
