The intake form captures one idea. The inventory is the register that survives the review meeting. It records what the agency knows about each AI use case: who owns it, which department it affects, its current tier, its status, what data it touches, and when it needs review again.

This page provides a lightweight browser-only starter inventory for agencies that do not yet have a portfolio tool. It is intentionally simple: add reviewed ideas, export CSV or Markdown, and move the export into the agency system of record.

> **Use this as your operating inventory.** An inventory is a core Phase 1 control because it keeps AI work visible after review. Some federal, state, or grant-specific inventories require more fields or a prescribed format; use this starter structure to get organized, then expand it when counsel or the reporting body identifies required fields.

## How this page scales for your agency

| Path | Minimum useful version | Add when capacity exists |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Small / no IT department | Track use case, sponsor, department, tier, status, data category, and next review in the browser tool or a spreadsheet | Add vendor/system name and impact-assessment status for anything public-facing or sensitive |
| Medium | Export CSV after each review meeting and store it with committee minutes | Add procurement status, approved tool, and decision-support fields |
| Large | Maintain the inventory in the agency portfolio/GRC system with records retention and reporting views | Add state/federal reporting fields, impact assessments, public inventory fields, and audit history |

## Minimum fields

| Field | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Use case | Human-readable name used in meeting minutes and reports |
| Sponsor | Person accountable for the workflow and follow-up |
| Department | Where the idea originated and who will pilot it |
| Tier | Current risk classification from the Review Committee |
| Status | Submitted, approved, deferred, retired, or needs refinement |
| Vendor / system | Tool, vendor, or internally built system involved |
| Data category | Public, internal, sensitive, regulated, or unknown |
| Decision support | Whether AI independently makes, informs, or materially supports a decision |
| Impact assessment | Not needed, needed, in progress, complete, or counsel review |
| Next review | Date the committee rechecks status, controls, or law changes |

## Operating rule

The inventory is updated only after committee review. Staff should submit ideas through the [AI Use Case Intake Form](/phase-2-education/use-case-intake/); the program lead then copies approved or deferred ideas into the inventory after the tier is confirmed.
