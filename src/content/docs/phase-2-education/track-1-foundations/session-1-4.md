The final session of Track 1 closes the loop: every attendee converts the task they identified in Session 1.3 into a real intake submission. By the end of the 90 minutes, the Review Committee has a fresh queue of use cases to triage.

## Learning objectives

By the end of this session, every attendee can:

1. Use the AI Use Case Intake Form to submit an idea.
2. Explain — in one sentence — what the 3-tier risk model is and why their use case landed where it did.
3. Describe what happens after they submit (timelines, sponsor liaison, possible outcomes).

## Audience

Same as Sessions 1.1–1.3. Pre-requisite: Sessions 1.1 and 1.3 (1.2 strongly recommended).

## Materials

- AI deck source markdown: [Download the deck source](/deck-sources/phase-2/track-1-foundations/session-1-4-from-idea-to-intake.md). Paste or upload it into your preferred AI presentation tool, then localize, verify, and brand the generated deck before use.
- Live access to the [AI Use Case Intake Form](/phase-2-education/use-case-intake/) — every attendee on a laptop or with a partner who has one.
- Live access to the [Risk Tier Picker](/phase-1-governance/risk-classification/) — for the classification activity.
- Each attendee's "My Job + AI" worksheet from Session 1.3.
- Printed handout: who to email the exported intake to (the agency-specific intake mailbox or SharePoint location).
- Track 1 final quiz (15 questions, paper or digital, covering Sessions 1.1–1.4).

## 30-minute presentation

1. **Recap of the three-tier model** (5 min). Tier 1, 2, 3 — what each means in plain language. Show the matrix from the [Risk Classification](/phase-1-governance/risk-classification/) page on screen. Don't go through every dimension; emphasize the "rule of thumb" line: if a real person can lose a benefit, job, custody, housing, freedom, or money because of the AI's output, it's High.
2. **Live demo of the intake form** (10 min). Walk through the form with a real example — ideally a use case that came up during Session 1.3 group discussion. Show how filling in the audience, decision impact, and data fields produces a provisional tier. Show the export and what the markdown looks like.
3. **What happens after you submit** (5 min). Email the file to the intake mailbox. Initial tier proposed by the AI program lead. Review Committee confirms or escalates at the next standing meeting. You'll hear back according to the agency's published response target; 15 business days is a useful example, not a universal rule.
4. **Common intake mistakes** (5 min). Five quick examples from the [intake page](/phase-2-education/use-case-intake/) — too broad, no measurable outcome, solution looking for a problem, vendor as sponsor, data sensitivity left blank. Reading these aloud beats hearing them in the rejection email.
5. **The committee is on your side** (5 min). The committee's job is not to say no. Most submissions get approved, often with adjustments. The committee's job is to make the path from idea to deployment safe. Frame this clearly — staff who think the committee is a gate submit fewer ideas.

## 30-minute hands-on intake submission

Every attendee:

1. **Opens the intake form** on their laptop. (Pair attendees without laptops with someone who has one.)
2. **Picks a task from their My Job + AI worksheet** — ideally a ⭐⭐⭐ from Session 1.3. If they don't have one, the facilitator suggests one based on their role.
3. **Fills in the form completely** — name, sponsor, problem, audience, decision, data. The provisional tier appears.
4. **Discusses the tier with their partner** — does it feel right? Would the committee likely confirm or escalate?
5. **Exports the markdown** and emails it to the intake mailbox during the session. Submitted in the room is much higher conversion than "submitted later this week."

The facilitator circulates, helps with field clarification, and notes patterns (everyone in the same role family submitting the same idea is signal — that's a candidate Domain Lab topic).

## 20-minute knowledge check + final discussion

The 15-question Track 1 quiz, taken individually. Covers vocabulary (Session 1.1), risk patterns (Session 1.2), augmentation vs. automation (Session 1.3), and the intake / tiering process (Session 1.4).

Pass threshold: 80%. Below 80%, the facilitator offers a 30-minute office hour the following week — privately, no public reporting. Attendees who score 100% are invited (not pressured) to nominate themselves for the Champions Network (Track 5).

After the quiz, run the closing share-out:

- Each attendee names the use case they just submitted, in one sentence.
- The facilitator reads back patterns: "Three of you submitted summarization use cases. Two submitted translation. One submitted a Tier-3 you should hear about at the deep-dive." This previews the committee's reality.

## 10-minute closing

Wrap-up: the committee meets on the agency's standing cadence. Submissions made today go into the next review cycle. Each attendee will hear back according to the local response target. The Champions Network onboards next quarter; nominations are open.

Thank the room. Track 1 is complete for this cohort.

## Common questions and how to handle them

- **"What if I'm wrong about the tier?"** "The provisional tier is a starting point. The committee makes the final call, and they may adjust based on context only they have. Wrong-but-submitted is much better than right-but-not-submitted. Submit the form."
- **"Can I submit more than one?"** "Yes. We'd rather have three rough ideas than one polished one. The committee can triage."
- **"What if my idea overlaps with someone else's?"** "Submit anyway. Overlap is signal that the committee should consolidate or pick a single sponsor. They'll handle the merging."
- **"Will I be told if my idea is rejected?"** "You'll be told the decision either way, with the rationale, according to the local response target. Rejections are rare; 'hold for later' is more common, and that's not a no — that's 'we need additional controls before we can deploy this safely.'"
- **"Can I take this idea outside the program?"** "If you mean: 'can I just go use a consumer AI tool with our data?' — no. That's an AUP violation. If you mean: 'can I keep working on it informally' — yes, but the committee has resources you don't, and you'll move faster through them. Submit it."

## Async fallback

- 18-minute video covering the presentation portion plus a recorded intake-form walkthrough.
- Self-paced intake submission — completed at the attendee's pace and emailed in.
- 15-question quiz, auto-graded.
- Optional 15-minute conversation with a Champion or the AI program lead within two weeks of submission.

## Evaluation

- **Level 1.** Post-session 5-question survey. Track 1 cumulative target 4.2/5.0.
- **Level 2.** Final quiz score. Target 80% pass rate.
- **Level 3.** Submitted intake forms (counted at session and 30 days post). Target ≥50% of cohort submits ≥1 within 30 days.
- **Level 4.** Cohort intake submission rate vs. departments without Track 1 delivered. Departments with Track 1 should produce 5–10× more intake than those without.

## What to leave out

- Detailed walk-through of every Tier-3 disclosure requirement (that's Track 3).
- Vendor evaluation (also Track 3).
- Full impact assessment methodology (Tier-3 deep-dive territory).
- Detailed instructions for staff who want to be on the Review Committee — handle those individually after the session.
