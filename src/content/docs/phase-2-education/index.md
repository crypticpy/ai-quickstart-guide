## Objective

Build AI literacy across the organization so every role — technical and non-technical — understands what AI can do, what it can't, and how to contribute to the agency's AI strategy.

## Seven training tracks

| #   | Track                     | Audience            | Duration        | Timing      |
| --- | ------------------------- | ------------------- | --------------- | ----------- |
| 1   | AI Foundations            | All staff           | 4 sessions      | Months 1–2  |
| 2   | Leadership & Strategy     | Directors, CxOs     | 4 briefings     | Months 1–3  |
| 3   | Governance & Compliance   | Policy, legal, CISO | 5 sessions      | Months 1–3  |
| 4   | Developer Upskilling      | IT / engineering    | 8 labs          | Months 4–7  |
| 5   | AI Champions Network      | Cross-dept leads    | Ongoing monthly | Months 2–12 |
| 6   | Domain Expert Labs        | Program staff       | Ongoing monthly | Months 3–12 |
| 7   | Middle Manager Enablement | Supervisors         | 3 sessions      | Months 2–4  |

Detailed session guides for each track ship in Sprints 2 and 4.

## Deliverables

- Curriculum map (this directory)
- AI use case intake form (lightweight, 5 questions)
- Change management playbook (ADKAR-based)
- Job-impact messaging kit
- Sustainability playbook (months 7–12+)
