Government procurement cycles typically need 12–18 months of lead time. Agencies reading this guide today are most likely budgeting for FY2027 or FY2028. This checklist is what to do _before_ the budget exists — the free and low-cost actions that position the agency to make a credible, specific budget request when the cycle opens, instead of asking for "AI money" with nothing to point to.

Everything below is achievable without procurement. None of it requires new staff, new tools, or a contract.

## Pre-funding actions

- [ ] Complete the [Readiness Assessment](/getting-started/readiness-assessment/) and record the score.
- [ ] Draft the AUP from the [Acceptable Use Policy template](/phase-1-governance/acceptable-use-policy/), adapted for the agency's context.
- [ ] Draft the 3-tier [risk classification](/phase-1-governance/risk-classification/) with examples mapped to actual agency operations.
- [ ] Charter the [AI Review Committee](/phase-1-governance/review-committee/) — name members, decision rules, meeting cadence.
- [ ] Attach the [procurement addendum](/phase-1-governance/procurement-guardrails/) to existing standard contract templates so the next AI-adjacent contract picks it up automatically.
- [ ] Run [Track 1 (AI Literacy)](/phase-2-education/track-1-foundations/) facilitator-led from the curriculum map — no LMS required; existing meeting rooms and a slide deck are enough.
- [ ] Stand up the [use case intake form](/phase-2-education/use-case-intake/) and start collecting candidate pilots from staff.
- [ ] Identify 2–3 [champion volunteers](/phase-2-education/track-5-champions/) — people who already experiment with AI on their own time and will form the seed network.
- [ ] Complete the cost worksheet in the [ROI calculator](/resources/roi-calculator/) to size the FY2027 / FY2028 budget ask with line-item specificity.

## What this positions you for

Once the checklist is complete, the agency has an adopted policy, a chartered committee, a trained baseline of staff, a real backlog of use cases, and a sized cost model. A budget request grounded in those artifacts can credibly include:

- A cloud sandbox tier and the managed AI services that run on it (Phase 3).
- A Track 4 developer cohort — internal upskilling rather than external hires (Phase 2).
- A contract developer or first dedicated FTE to build the platform's first three modules (Phase 5).
- Procurement of foundation-model access (Bedrock, Azure OpenAI, Vertex AI, or third-party API) under terms the procurement addendum already covers.

The asks are concrete, sequenced, and tied to deliverables the agency has already started — which is the difference between an AI budget request that lands and one that gets cut.
