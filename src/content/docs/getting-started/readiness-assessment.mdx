Twelve questions, two per domain. Takes about five minutes. Your answers stay in your browser — nothing is sent anywhere — and you can export a Markdown summary or print to PDF when you're done.

> **Why start here.** The assessment outputs a maturity level (Crawl / Walk / Run / Fly) and a per-domain score. That's the input to picking which phase you should focus on next. An agency at "Walk" in governance but "Crawl" in infrastructure has a very different next 90 days than one with the reverse.

## What the score means

| Total | Level | Where to focus next |
|---|---|---|
| 0–11 | 1 — Crawl | Phase 1 (Governance) and Track 1 (AI Foundations training) |
| 12–23 | 2 — Walk | Phase 3 (Infrastructure) once governance is adopted; begin Track 4 (Developer Upskilling) |
| 24–29 | 3 — Run | Phase 5 (Modular Platform) — you have the foundation to start building reusable modules |
| 30–36 | 4 — Fly | Phase 6 (Starter Project) — pick an archetype and ship; consider inner-source contributions |

## After the assessment

1. Save or print the results. Most agencies share them with their executive sponsor before kicking off Phase 1.
2. Look at the **lowest-scoring domain** — that's almost always the right place to invest first, regardless of your overall level.
3. Read the [Quickstart Checklist](/getting-started/quickstart-checklist/) for the week-by-week plan that gets you to the next maturity level.

## Re-take it quarterly

The assessment is meant to be retaken every 90 days during active implementation. Progress shows up as movement on the per-domain scores. If a domain isn't moving, that's a signal to escalate or rebalance.
