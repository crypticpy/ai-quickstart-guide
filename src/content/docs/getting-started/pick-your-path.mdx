## Small agency path: month-by-month

For agencies with very small IT teams the full 6-phase, 12-month plan is not realistic. The Small path focuses on what delivers value fastest and treats each milestone as a complete, defensible deliverable on its own.

- **Months 1–3, governance sprint plus AI foundations.** Hire external counsel for a 2-day governance intensive ($3–5K) that produces the AUP, risk matrix, and committee charter. Run Track 1 for all staff in parallel. This alone is a complete outcome that satisfies most legislative AI policy requirements.
- **Months 4–6, champions network plus vendor evaluation.** Launch monthly champions meetups. Instead of building infrastructure from scratch, evaluate managed AI services (Azure AI Services, AWS Bedrock, Google Vertex) using the vendor evaluation framework in the guide.
- **Months 7–12, managed platform plus simplified starter project.** Use cloud-managed services instead of custom modules. Build 1–3 lightweight modules (Auth, API wrapper, AI orchestration) on top of managed infrastructure. Deploy one starter project using pre-built frameworks (LangChain, Semantic Kernel, etc.).
- **Months 13–18, iterate and expand.** With the first app in production, iterate based on real usage. Add modules as needed. Consider hiring 1–2 contract engineers for month 13+ to build out the platform further.

> **Phase B checkpoint (Month 9).** At the end of Phase B, small agencies should evaluate whether they have the capacity and budget to continue to Phase C. If not, the governance framework, training, and 3-module foundation already delivered remain valid on their own; the agency can resume at Phase C when resources allow without redoing earlier work.

> **Off-Ramp: Governance only.** Agencies that complete only the Governance Sprint (Months 1–3) still walk away with an adopted AI policy, risk classification, and review committee. That is a complete, standalone deliverable that satisfies legislative requirements and positions the agency for future phases when budget and staffing allow.

## Year-1 budget anchor

Earlier drafts of this guide listed per-phase dollar ranges in a table here. Those rolled up to the same headline numbers shown in the variant table above (Small $50K to $150K, Standard $150K to $400K, Large $400K to $1M+) but did not show how the totals were built.

The headline ranges are operational, not procurement-cycle. They cover four cost categories:

1. People (largest cost: 60 to 75% of Year 1 in most cases)
2. Cloud and AI services
3. Tooling and licenses
4. External services (legal counsel, training facilitation, audits)

For sourced inputs (BLS pay data, OPM pay tables, current AI service pricing) and a downloadable spreadsheet template, see [Budget Methodology](/resources/budget-methodology/). The methodology page rebuilds these ranges from cited inputs so a finance team can replace defaults with their own pay bands.

> **Procurement reality check.** Government procurement cycles typically require 12 to 18 months of lead time. Agencies reading this today are likely budgeting for FY2027 or FY2028. Use the free or low-cost Phase 1 governance resources immediately while you position for budget requests.

## Where to go next

Once you've picked a path, jump to the phase that fits your current state. Your selection follows you:

- [Phase 1: Governance](/phase-1-governance/). Every agency starts here.
- [Phase 2: Culture & Education](/phase-2-education/). Tracks 1, 5, 7 are the small-agency minimum (plus a 2-day Track 3 governance sprint).
- [Phase 3: Infrastructure](/phase-3-infrastructure/). Small path leans on managed services; Standard/Large run the full sandbox.
- [Phase 4: Dev Stack](/phase-4-dev-stack/). Coding standards and AI-assisted development.
- [Phase 5: Platform](/phase-5-platform/). Small path uses 1–3 lightweight modules on managed infra.
- [Phase 6: Starter Project](/phase-6-starter-projects/). Pick a low-stakes archetype and ship.

If you haven't taken the [Readiness Assessment](/getting-started/readiness-assessment/) yet, do that first. Its score is the input to choosing where in your path to focus.
