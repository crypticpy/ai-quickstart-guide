# Remote Work Policy

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-HR-014, effective 2024-07-01

## Eligibility

Permanent staff in classifications below grade 22 may request a remote work arrangement after completing six full months of service. Probationary employees, field inspectors assigned to in-person posts, and front-counter clerks are not eligible. Eligibility is reviewed annually at the start of the fiscal year.

## Schedule patterns

Three patterns are approved.

1. Hybrid two days remote per week.
2. Hybrid three days remote per week with a fixed in-office anchor day.
3. Fully remote, available only to staff whose duties have been formally documented as location-independent by the division director.

Anchor days for pattern 2 are Tuesday or Wednesday. Friday remote work is allowed only for patterns 1 and 3.

## Equipment

The Department issues a laptop, headset, and docking station to every approved remote worker. Personal devices may not be used to access internal systems. Staff who need a second monitor at home may request one through the IT service portal; requests are filled within ten business days subject to inventory.

## Time tracking and core hours

Remote workers maintain core hours of 9:30 AM to 3:30 PM local time. Core hours apply regardless of timezone if the employee has been approved for out-of-state work. Time is recorded in the standard timesheet system; remote work does not change overtime rules.

## Information security

Remote workers complete the annual information security refresher in addition to the standard onboarding course. Remote sessions go through the Department's VPN. Public records and personally identifiable information may not be printed at home.

## Termination of arrangement

A remote arrangement may be terminated by the employee with two weeks notice or by the supervisor with thirty days notice and written justification. The supervisor's decision is appealable to the division director and then to Human Resources.
