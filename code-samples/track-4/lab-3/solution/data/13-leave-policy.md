# Leave Policy

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-HR-006, effective 2024-01-01

## Annual leave

Permanent staff accrue annual leave based on years of service.

- Years 0 to 5: 12 days per year.
- Years 6 to 10: 18 days per year.
- Years 11 and above: 24 days per year.

Unused annual leave carries over up to a cap of 40 days. Leave above the cap is forfeited at the end of the calendar year unless the supervisor denied a request that would have brought the balance under the cap.

## Sick leave

Permanent staff accrue 12 sick days per year. Sick leave does not have a cap and does not expire. On separation, unused sick leave is not paid out, except as required by a collective bargaining agreement.

## Family and medical leave

Eligible staff may take up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave under federal Family and Medical Leave Act rules. The Department supplements the federal leave with up to 4 weeks of paid parental leave for the birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child.

## Bereavement

Staff may take up to 5 paid days of bereavement leave for the death of an immediate family member and up to 2 paid days for an extended family member.

## Jury duty and witness leave

Staff are paid in full while serving on a jury or appearing as a witness under subpoena in their official capacity. Jury checks are signed over to the Department.

## Military leave

Staff who are members of the National Guard or Reserves receive paid military leave consistent with state law. Active duty deployments follow USERRA reemployment rules.

## Approval

Requests for annual leave are submitted at least three business days in advance through the time and attendance system. Sick leave is reported as soon as practicable on the day of absence. Extended leave of more than five consecutive days requires supervisor approval and may require medical documentation.
