# Grants Management

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-FIN-019, effective 2024-02-15

## Pre-award

A grant opportunity is reviewed by the Finance division and the General Counsel before submission. The review confirms that the Department can meet match requirements, period of performance, and reporting obligations. Submissions go through the Department head or a delegated signatory.

## Award acceptance

A new award is accepted in writing by the Department head or delegate. Award terms, including indirect cost rate, allowable costs, and reporting deadlines, are recorded in the grants system within ten business days of acceptance.

## Subrecipient monitoring

Pass-through awards include a written subaward agreement that names the subrecipient, the scope, the period of performance, and the reporting cadence. The Department conducts a risk assessment of each subrecipient and applies monitoring activities proportional to the assessed risk. Monitoring includes desk reviews of submitted reports and on-site reviews for high-risk subrecipients.

## Time and effort

Staff funded in whole or in part by a grant complete a monthly time and effort certification that documents the percentage of time worked on each funding source. Certifications are reviewed and approved by the supervisor.

## Reporting

Financial and programmatic reports are submitted by the deadlines in the award. Late reports are escalated to the division director.

## Closeout

Grant closeout occurs within ninety days of the end of the period of performance. Closeout includes final financial report, final programmatic report, equipment disposition, and records consolidation.

## Audits

Grant records are subject to single audit and to grantor audits. Records are retained for the longer of seven years or the period required by the awarding agency.
