# Social Media

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-COMM-007, effective 2024-06-01

## Purpose

The Department uses social media to share public information, announce services, and engage with the community. This policy covers the operation of official Department accounts and the personal use of social media by staff.

## Official accounts

Official accounts are owned by the Department, not by individual staff. Account credentials are stored in the Department password manager. New accounts require approval from the Communications division and the Office of the Chief Information Officer. Accounts that have not posted in six months are reviewed for retirement.

## Content

Posts on official accounts are factual, neutral, and consistent with Department messaging. Posts about ongoing investigations, personnel matters, or pending litigation are coordinated with the General Counsel before publication.

## Comments and moderation

Comments on official accounts are moderated under a published moderation policy. Comments that contain threats, personal attacks, defamation, sexually explicit content, or commercial spam are removed. Moderation actions are logged. Members of the public who are blocked may appeal to the Communications division.

## Records

Posts and direct messages on official accounts are public records and are captured by the Department's social media archiving service. Records are retained according to the Department records retention schedule.

## Personal accounts

Staff using personal social media accounts make clear that they speak only for themselves. Staff do not disclose non-public Department information on personal accounts. Staff do not post in a way that would discredit the Department or undermine public trust in their official duties.

## Incident response

A compromised official account is reported to the security operations team within one hour. The Communications division coordinates public messaging during a compromise.
