# Code of Conduct

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-HR-001, effective 2023-09-01

## Purpose

The Department serves the public. Every staff member is a steward of public trust. This code sets the minimum standard for conduct at work and in any setting where staff represent the Department.

## Honesty and integrity

Staff are truthful in their official statements, records, and reports. Falsifying a record, a timesheet, or an expense claim is grounds for discipline up to and including termination.

## Respect

Staff treat colleagues, members of the public, and partners with respect. Harassment, intimidation, retaliation, and discriminatory behavior are prohibited. Reports may be filed with Human Resources or with the Office of the Inspector General. Reports are handled confidentially to the extent allowed by law.

## Conflicts of interest

Staff disclose personal or financial interests that could affect their official duties. Staff do not accept gifts from current or prospective vendors or from members of the public seeking a permit, license, or grant. Modest refreshments at public meetings are not gifts.

## Use of position

Staff do not use their position for private gain. Staff do not use Department resources, including time, equipment, and information, for outside employment, political activity, or personal business.

## Confidential information

Staff protect confidential information learned through their duties. This obligation continues after employment ends.

## Outside employment

Staff may hold outside employment that does not conflict with their Department duties. Outside employment is reported in writing to the supervisor and to Human Resources. Outside employment that involves the Department's vendors, applicants, or grantees requires written approval from the division director.

## Reporting violations

Suspected violations are reported to the supervisor, to Human Resources, or anonymously to the Inspector General hotline. The Department prohibits retaliation against staff who report in good faith.
