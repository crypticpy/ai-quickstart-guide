# Public Records Request Procedure

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-LEG-005, effective 2024-03-01

## Submitting a request

A public records request may be submitted in writing by mail, by email to records@example.gov, or through the Department's online intake form. The Department does not require a stated reason for a request. The requester does not need to be a resident of the jurisdiction.

## Timeline

The Department acknowledges receipt of a request within five business days. A complete response is provided within fifteen business days. If the request requires search or review across multiple divisions, the Department may extend the deadline by up to ten additional business days with written notice to the requester.

## Fees

The first hour of search and review time is free. After the first hour, the Department charges $35 per hour for staff time. Reproduction is $0.15 per single-sided black-and-white page and $0.75 per color page. Electronic delivery in the original format is free where feasible. Fees may be waived in full or in part if the request is in the public interest, at the discretion of the General Counsel.

## Redaction

Records may be redacted to remove information protected by statute, including home addresses of public employees, juvenile records, and active investigation files. Each redaction is logged with a citation to the statute that supports it.

## Denials

If a record is withheld in full, the Department issues a written denial that lists the records withheld in general terms and the legal basis for the withholding. The requester may appeal a denial to the Department head within thirty days. Denials and appeals are reported to the State Records Council on a quarterly basis.

## Tracking

Every request is logged in the public records tracking system. The log captures intake date, requester contact information, scope, due date, fee assessment, and final disposition. Logs are retained for ten years.
