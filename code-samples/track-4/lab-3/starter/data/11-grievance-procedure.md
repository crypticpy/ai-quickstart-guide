# Grievance Procedure

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-HR-018, effective 2023-11-01

## Purpose

This procedure provides a path for staff to raise and resolve workplace concerns that are not covered by a separate complaint process such as harassment, discrimination, or a labor grievance under a collective bargaining agreement.

## Step 1: Informal resolution

The staff member raises the concern with the immediate supervisor. The supervisor responds within five business days. If the concern is with the supervisor, the staff member may go directly to step 2.

## Step 2: Written grievance

If informal resolution does not solve the issue, the staff member files a written grievance with the division director within fifteen business days of the events at issue. The grievance describes the issue, the steps already taken, and the requested remedy. The division director responds in writing within ten business days.

## Step 3: Department head review

If the staff member is not satisfied with the division director's response, the staff member may appeal to the Department head within ten business days of the response. The Department head reviews the file, may meet with the parties, and issues a written decision within twenty business days. The Department head's decision is final at the Department level.

## Step 4: External review

Where allowed by statute or by a collective bargaining agreement, the staff member may appeal to the relevant external body, including the State Personnel Board, within thirty days of the Department head's decision.

## Confidentiality

Grievance files are confidential. Information is shared only with those who need it to investigate or to act.

## No retaliation

The Department prohibits retaliation against any staff member who files a grievance in good faith or who participates in the investigation of one.

## Records

Grievance files are retained for ten years.
