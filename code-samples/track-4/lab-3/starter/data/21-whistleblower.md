# Whistleblower Protections

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-LEG-009, effective 2023-05-01

## Purpose

This policy protects staff who, in good faith, report a violation of law, a misuse of public resources, a substantial and specific danger to public health or safety, or an act of gross mismanagement.

## Reporting channels

A protected report may be made to the supervisor, to the Department head, to the Inspector General hotline, or to the State Auditor. Reports may be made anonymously through the hotline or the Auditor.

## Protections

Staff who make a protected report in good faith are protected from retaliation, including discipline, demotion, transfer, denial of promotion, and harassment. Protections apply even if the underlying report is later found to be unsubstantiated, as long as the report was made in good faith.

## Investigation

The Inspector General investigates protected reports within the Department. Investigations are conducted independently from operational management. Findings are reported to the Department head and, where appropriate, to outside authorities.

## Confidentiality

The identity of a reporter is kept confidential to the extent allowed by law. Confidentiality may be limited if the report leads to a disciplinary action or to litigation, in which case the reporter is notified before identity is disclosed.

## Cooperation

Staff cooperate with investigations and provide truthful information. Obstructing an investigation is itself a violation of policy.

## Posting

This policy is posted in common areas and on the staff intranet. The hotline number is included in the annual code of conduct training.

## Records

Whistleblower files are retained for ten years.
