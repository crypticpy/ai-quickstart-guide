# Records Retention Schedule

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-RM-002, effective 2023-01-15

## Purpose

This schedule sets the minimum and maximum retention periods for records created or received by the Department. It applies to records in any format including paper, email, cloud storage, and database rows.

## Retention periods

Permits and licensing case files are retained for seven years after the case is closed.

Personnel files are retained for the duration of employment plus seventy-five years.

Procurement files for purchases under $50,000 are retained for three years from the close of the fiscal year in which the purchase was made. Files for purchases of $50,000 and above are retained for seven years.

General correspondence not tied to a case is retained for two years.

Internal meeting minutes are retained for five years.

Email retention defaults to three years from the date the message was sent or received. Individual records that fall under another category in this schedule follow the longer period.

Drafts and working copies of public-facing documents may be deleted once the final version has been published, except where a draft is itself a record under the public records act.

## Disposition

Records reaching the end of their retention period are dispositioned according to the schedule. Routine records are deleted. Records of historical or cultural value are transferred to the Department archives. Litigation holds suspend disposition until counsel releases the hold in writing.

## Annual review

Each division designates a records officer who reviews the schedule annually with the Department records administrator. Changes to the schedule require sign-off from the General Counsel and the records administrator.

## Records covered by other authorities

Some records, including financial audit work papers and grant records, are governed by federal retention rules. Where a federal rule sets a longer period, the longer period applies.
