# Procurement Thresholds

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-FIN-008, effective 2024-01-01

## Micro-purchases

Purchases under $5,000 are micro-purchases. They may be made on a Department purchase card by any cardholder, with a receipt uploaded within five business days. No solicitation is required. Splitting a larger purchase into multiple micro-purchases to avoid the threshold is prohibited.

## Small purchases

Purchases from $5,000 up to $50,000 require three written quotes from qualified vendors when feasible. The buyer documents each quote in the procurement system, including price, scope, and lead time. The selection memo notes which quote was chosen and why.

## Formal solicitation

Purchases of $50,000 and above require a formal solicitation. The default solicitation type is a request for proposals. The Department may use an invitation for bid for goods and routine services where the specifications are fixed and price is the determining factor. Solicitations are posted to the public procurement portal for at least thirty calendar days, except for emergency procurements.

## Sole source

A sole source procurement is allowed only when one vendor is the unique provider of the good or service, the procurement is for follow-on work that requires the original vendor, or there is a documented emergency. Each sole source memo is signed by the division director and the Department procurement officer and posted to the public procurement portal.

## Cooperative purchasing

The Department may purchase off of approved state and federal cooperative contracts at any threshold. The cooperative contract number is recorded in the requisition.

## Conflicts of interest

Buyers and selection committee members complete an annual conflict of interest disclosure. A buyer who has a personal or financial relationship with a vendor recuses from any selection involving that vendor.
