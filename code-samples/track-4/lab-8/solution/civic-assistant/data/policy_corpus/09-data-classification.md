# Data Classification

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-IT-009, effective 2023-06-01

## Classes

The Department uses four data classes.

Public. Information cleared for release without restriction. Examples: published meeting agendas, posted job openings, public dashboards.

Internal. Information for staff and authorized partners. Examples: internal training materials, working drafts, routine internal email.

Confidential. Information whose disclosure would harm the Department or an individual. Examples: personnel records, internal investigations, vendor pricing during a procurement.

Restricted. Information whose disclosure is restricted by law or contract. Examples: protected health information, juvenile records, criminal justice information, federal tax information.

## Handling

Public data may be stored or transmitted without additional controls.

Internal data is stored in approved Department systems and shared with staff and named partners on a need-to-know basis.

Confidential data is encrypted at rest in approved systems. Access is logged. Sharing outside the Department requires a written agreement.

Restricted data is encrypted in transit and at rest. Access is logged and reviewed quarterly. Restricted data is processed only in systems that have completed the relevant compliance review.

## Labeling

Documents and emails carry a class label in the footer. Database tables carry a class tag in their schema metadata. Where a record contains data from more than one class, the highest class applies.

## Reclassification

Data is reclassified by the data owner with concurrence from the Department privacy officer. Public release of previously confidential data follows the public records request procedure.

## Disposal

Data is disposed of according to the records retention schedule. Restricted data is disposed of using approved sanitization methods recorded in a sanitization log.
