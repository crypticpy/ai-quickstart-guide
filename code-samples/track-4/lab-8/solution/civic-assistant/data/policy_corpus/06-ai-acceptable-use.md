# Acceptable Use Policy for AI Tools

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-IT-031, effective 2025-02-01

## Scope

This policy covers the use of generative AI tools, including chat interfaces, code assistants, and embedded AI features in commercial software. It applies to all staff, contractors, and interns when acting on behalf of the Department.

## Approved tools

Only AI tools listed on the Department's approved tools register may be used with Department information. The register is maintained by the Office of the Chief Information Officer and updated quarterly. Use of unapproved tools, including consumer accounts of approved vendors, is not permitted for Department work.

## Sensitive data

Staff may not paste personally identifiable information, sealed records, attorney work product, or non-public investigative information into any AI tool unless that tool has been certified for the relevant data class. The approved tools register lists the data class certifications for each tool.

## Disclosure

Any document, email, or analysis where AI played a substantive role in drafting carries a footer that says the work was AI-assisted and was reviewed by a named staff member. Routine grammar checks and short summaries of public meeting minutes do not require disclosure.

## Verification

Staff verify AI output before relying on it. Citations from AI tools are checked against the cited source. Numerical claims are recomputed from the underlying data. AI-generated code is reviewed and tested before merge.

## Training

All staff complete the AI fundamentals course before using any AI tool for Department work. Staff in roles with elevated risk, including legal, human resources, and procurement, complete an additional role-specific module.

## Reporting incidents

Any suspected leak of sensitive data to an AI tool is reported to the security operations team within one business hour through the standard incident channel.
