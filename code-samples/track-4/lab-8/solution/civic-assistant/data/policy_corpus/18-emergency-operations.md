# Emergency Operations

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-OPS-003, effective 2024-04-01

## Activation

The Department activates its emergency operations plan when the jurisdiction declares an emergency, when continuity of operations is at risk, or when directed by the Department head. Activation is announced through the Department-wide alert system.

## Levels

Three activation levels.

Level 1 is monitoring. Designated coordinators monitor the situation and report status twice daily.

Level 2 is partial activation. Key staff report to the Department operations center on a defined schedule. Routine work continues for staff not directly engaged.

Level 3 is full activation. The Department operations center is staffed around the clock. Routine work is paused for activated staff.

## Roles

The Department head names an incident commander for each activation. The incident commander reports to the Department head and coordinates with the jurisdiction emergency operations center. Section chiefs cover operations, planning, logistics, and finance.

## Communications

Activation status is posted on the staff intranet and is sent through the alert system. Public communications go through the Communications division and align with messaging from the jurisdiction emergency operations center.

## Continuity

Each division maintains a continuity plan that lists essential functions, the staff who can perform them, the systems they need, and a forty-eight hour, two-week, and thirty-day recovery target. Plans are exercised at least annually.

## After-action

Each activation closes with an after-action review within thirty days. The review covers timeline, decisions, what worked, what did not, and three to five action items with owners and due dates.
