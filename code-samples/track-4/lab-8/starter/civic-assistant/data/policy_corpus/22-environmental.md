# Environmental Sustainability

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-FAC-005, effective 2024-08-15

## Goals

The Department reduces its operational footprint each year against a fiscal year 2022 baseline. Targets cover energy use, water use, fleet emissions, and waste diversion. Targets and progress are published on the public sustainability dashboard.

## Buildings

New construction and major renovations meet at least the LEED Silver standard. Operating buildings are benchmarked annually using the Energy Star Portfolio Manager and reported to the Office of the Chief Operating Officer.

## Fleet

The Department transitions its fleet to zero-emission vehicles where mission-suitable. Light-duty replacements default to zero-emission unless a documented need supports a different choice. Heavy-duty replacements follow the public works fleet plan. Idling of Department vehicles is limited to five minutes except where an active operational need requires otherwise.

## Procurement

Procurements include sustainability criteria where appropriate. Paper purchases default to recycled content. Electronics purchases default to EPEAT-registered models. Single-use plastics are minimized at Department events.

## Waste

Department buildings provide recycling and compost collection. Waste audits are conducted annually in at least two buildings on a rotating basis. Audit findings inform the next year's contract scope.

## Reporting

Sustainability metrics are reported quarterly to the Department head and annually to the public.
