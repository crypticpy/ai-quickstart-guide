# Digital Accessibility

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-IT-022, effective 2024-09-01

## Standard

The Department's public-facing websites, applications, and electronic documents conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines version 2.2, level AA.

## Scope

The standard applies to any digital product made available to the public or to staff. New procurements include accessibility requirements in the solicitation. Vendor accessibility conformance reports are required at the time of contract award and reviewed annually.

## Testing

Each release of a public-facing application is tested with automated tooling and with at least one manual review using a screen reader. Significant releases are tested with users who rely on assistive technology. Testing results are recorded in the accessibility tracker.

## Documents

Documents posted to the public website are tagged for accessibility before posting. Scanned PDFs are not posted unless an accessible alternative is also posted at the same time.

## Procurement

Information technology procurements include the standard accessibility clause and require a Voluntary Product Accessibility Template or equivalent. Where a product does not fully conform, the vendor provides a remediation plan with milestones.

## Complaints

Members of the public may file an accessibility complaint through the public records request portal or by emailing accessibility@example.gov. Complaints are acknowledged within five business days and resolved within forty-five days. Complaints and resolutions are reported quarterly to the Office of the Chief Information Officer.

## Training

All staff who produce digital content complete the Department's accessibility training within thirty days of hire and every two years thereafter.
