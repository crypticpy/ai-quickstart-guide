# Volunteer Program

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-HR-022, effective 2023-08-01

## Program purpose

The Department engages volunteers to extend its service to the community. Volunteers do not replace paid staff and do not perform work that creates a conflict with collective bargaining agreements.

## Application and screening

Volunteers complete an application and a background check appropriate to the assignment. Volunteers who will work directly with children, with vulnerable adults, or with sensitive information complete the heightened background check.

## Onboarding

Each volunteer receives a written assignment description, a named staff supervisor, and an orientation that covers the code of conduct, reporting obligations, and safety. Volunteers sign a volunteer agreement before the first shift.

## Hours

A volunteer may work up to twenty hours per week. Hours above this require approval from the division director. Volunteers track hours in the volunteer system. Hours are reported quarterly to the Office of the Chief Operating Officer.

## Reimbursement

Volunteers may be reimbursed for pre-approved travel and incidental expenses at the Department's standard rates. Volunteers are not paid wages and do not accrue leave or benefits.

## Recognition

The Department recognizes volunteer contributions in the annual report and at an annual recognition event. Recognition does not include cash awards.

## Termination

A volunteer assignment may be ended by the volunteer at any time. The Department may end an assignment for cause, including conduct inconsistent with the code of conduct, or without cause when program needs change. The Department provides a written notice.

## Records

Volunteer records are retained for five years after the end of the assignment.
