# Language Access

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-OPS-018, effective 2024-02-01

## Purpose

The Department provides meaningful access to its programs and services for residents who have limited English proficiency.

## Languages covered

The Department's primary covered languages are determined annually based on census data and service utilization. The current covered languages are Spanish, Vietnamese, Mandarin, Korean, Tagalog, Arabic, and Amharic. Other languages are served on request through the contracted interpretation service.

## Vital documents

Vital documents are translated into all primary covered languages. Vital documents include applications for benefits, complaint forms, notices of rights, and key public-facing pages on the Department website.

## Interpretation

Telephonic interpretation is available at all public service points within sixty seconds of pickup. Front-counter staff are trained to identify a need for interpretation and to use the interpretation service. In-person interpretation is provided for scheduled appointments on at least two business days notice.

## Signage

Public service points display "I speak" cards listing the primary covered languages. Signage indicating the availability of free interpretation is posted in plain view at all public service points.

## Training

All public-facing staff complete the language access training within thirty days of hire and every two years thereafter.

## Complaints

Members of the public may file a language access complaint through the public records request portal or by emailing language-access@example.gov. Complaints are tracked, investigated, and resolved within thirty business days.

## Reporting

Language access metrics, including translated documents, interpretation calls, and complaint resolution, are reported annually to the Department head and to the public.
