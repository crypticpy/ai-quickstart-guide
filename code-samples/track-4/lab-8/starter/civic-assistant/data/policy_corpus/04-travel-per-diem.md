# Travel and Per Diem

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-FIN-021, effective 2024-10-01

## Authorization

All non-local travel requires written authorization in the travel system before any expense is incurred. Local travel within the metropolitan area is reimbursed at the standard mileage rate without prior authorization, up to a monthly cap of 200 miles.

## Per diem rates

Per diem covers meals and incidental expenses. The rate for travel within the state is $66 per full day. Out-of-state travel is reimbursed at the federal General Services Administration rate for the destination city. The first and last day of travel are reimbursed at 75 percent of the daily rate.

## Lodging

Lodging is reimbursed at actual cost up to the federal GSA lodging cap for the destination. Stays above the cap require pre-approval from the division director and a written justification. The Department does not pay for room upgrades, mini-bar charges, in-room movies, or laundry on trips of fewer than seven days.

## Air travel

Coach class is the standard. Business class is allowed only for international flights of more than eight scheduled hours and only with division director approval before booking. Travelers should use the Department's contracted travel agency where possible to capture government rates.

## Receipts

Receipts are required for any single expense of $25 or more. Receipts must show the vendor, date, amount, and items purchased. Credit card statements alone do not substitute for receipts.

## Submission and reimbursement

A travel expense report is submitted within ten business days of returning. Reports submitted more than thirty days after return are rejected and require a written explanation from the supervisor before they will be processed. Reimbursements are paid by direct deposit on the next regular payroll cycle.
