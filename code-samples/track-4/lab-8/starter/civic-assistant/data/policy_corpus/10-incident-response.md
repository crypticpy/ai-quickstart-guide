# Incident Response

Department of Civic Operations
Policy DCO-IT-014, effective 2024-08-01

## Definitions

A security incident is any event that compromises or reasonably could compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of Department information or systems. A privacy incident is a subset of security incident that involves personal information.

## Severity

Severity is rated on a scale of one to four.

Severity one is a confirmed compromise of restricted data or an outage that affects mission-critical services for more than thirty minutes. Severity two is suspected compromise or an outage that affects a large group of staff. Severity three is a localized issue that affects a small group. Severity four is a minor issue with no operational impact.

## Reporting

Staff who suspect an incident report through the help desk or by emailing soc@example.gov within one hour of discovery. The security operations team triages and assigns a severity within thirty minutes for severity one and two cases.

## Containment

The on-call security engineer leads containment. Affected accounts are locked. Affected hosts are isolated from the network. Backup snapshots are preserved for forensic review.

## Notification

For privacy incidents involving residents, the Department notifies affected individuals within forty-five days of confirmation, unless a law enforcement hold is in place. The Department notifies the Office of the Attorney General within ten business days of confirmation. Notification templates are maintained by the General Counsel.

## Post-incident review

Every severity one and severity two incident gets a written post-incident review within ten business days. The review covers timeline, root cause, what worked, what did not, and three action items with owners and due dates. Reviews are blameless and focus on systems, not individuals.

## Records

Incident records are retained for seven years.
